Where Creativity Meets Advocacy: Exploring Design's Role in Gender Equality.

The Need for Gender Inclusivity in Malawi's Graphic Design Industry

Graphic design in Malawi has traditionally been a male-dominated field, like many other professions. The 2024 Maso Awards, a popular annual event in Malawi, highlighted this trend, with 100 percent of the nominees in the graphic design category being men. This underscores the lack of women and girls in graphic design career.

The problem stems from the broader issue of women in technology. Since graphic design involves interacting with computers and other devices, many women and girls find it difficult to break into the field. However, I believe this challenge can be addressed if we put greater emphasis on encouraging and supporting women in technology.

Nonetheless, I believe a career in graphic design holds great potential to address gender inequality if pursued seriously. For instance, women have proven their effectiveness in decision-making bodies like parliament. If we believe that women can drive meaningful change when in positions of power, the same logic applies to graphic design.

Women, drawing from their lived experiences of stereotypes, can be at the forefront of challenging and dismantling these biases, often doing so more effectively than their male counterparts. By increasing their involvement in graphic design, we can expect visuals that not only challenge but also reshape gender stereotypes.

To girls and women with knowledge in the field, I believe it's important to start now rather than wait for the perfect moment. It's not about perfection but about taking action. Men in the field can also contribute by supporting gender inclusivity and mentoring young women.

It should be noted, however, that the future of gender representation in Malawi's graphic design industry looks promising. With increased awareness and advocacy, one example is Tech Pearl, which has been training girls in graphic design.
